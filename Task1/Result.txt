# Task 1 Analysis Results
======================================================================
Model: Cost-Timeline Optimization with 0-1 Programming
======================================================================

## 1. Key Assumptions
----------------------------------------------------------------------
1. **Payload Capacity**: Fixed at 150 tons per launch (constant)
2. **Launch Frequency**: Increases by 1 launch/year per base
3. **Capacity Constraint**: Maximum 200 launches/year per base
4. **Base Capacity (2050)**: Historical average + 20 launches/year
5. **Cost Parameters**:
   - Elevator (Apex->Moon transfer): $92,700/ton ($92.7/kg)
   - Rocket (Direct launch): $1,500,000/ton ($1,500/kg)
6. **AHP Preference**: a=5 (Time weight: 0.8333, Cost weight: 0.1667)
7. **Optimization**: Normalized weighted objective for base selection

## 2. Calculated Metrics
----------------------------------------------------------------------

### Scenario A: Space Elevator Only
  - Total Cost: $10,945,977,653,631 (10.95 Trillion USD)
  - Completion Time: 186.22 years (Complete by: 2236)
  - Initial Annual Capacity: 537,000 tons/year
  - Growth Model: Linear (constant capacity)

### Scenario B: Rockets Only (Optimized)
  - Total Cost: $150,572,654,407,500 (150.57 Trillion USD)
  - Completion Time: 392.77 years (Complete by: 2443)
  - Initial Annual Capacity: 52,950 tons/year
  - Selected Bases (10):
    • Florida
    • California

    • Mahia (NZ)
    • Kazakhstan
    • French Guiana
    • India
    • Virginia
    • Texas
    • Alaska
  - Growth Model: Piecewise (quadratic -> mixed -> linear)

### Scenario C: Hybrid (Synchronized)
  - Total Cost: $43,592,310,309,921 (43.59 Trillion USD)
  - Completion Time: 142.78 years (Complete by: 2193)
  - Initial Annual Capacity: 589,950 tons/year
  - Elevator Contribution: 0.77×10^8 tons
  - Rocket Contribution: 0.23×10^8 tons
  - Growth Model: Hybrid (elevator constant + rocket piecewise)

----------------------------------------------------------------------

  • Balanced cost-time trade-off
  • Risk diversification across two systems
  • Faster completion than elevator-only
